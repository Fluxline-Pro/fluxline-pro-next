[In Part 2: Discipline vs. Resonance](/blog/discipline-vs-resonance), we talked about how discipline is

Which brings us to your pathway: What IS discipline requiring of you? Why must
we constantly fight for what it is that we want? Maybe it becomes something
stronger and understood? Or why does it seem that so many closed doors prevent

Obstacles come dressed in daily life:

- Indecision
- Exhaustion
- Financial constraint
- Feedback loops from family, coworkers
- "Too much to do" and not enough space

These become the barriers that prevent you from staying disciplined AND from
detracting you from resonating with those things that bring you joy. Let's face
it: They're a pain and are hardly ever welcome in life.

But paradoxically, they also become the way forward. Or around. Or through. Or
backwards even! In short: _The obstacle becomes your path and as such, the way_.

## üß† Learning Through Friction

As you fail, try again, continue change, you find different ways to conquer your
obstacle. That's part of the process: _learning_. And with that friction comes
experience, resilience, determination, and courage... all of which are necessary
to push toward your goals.

From failing you learn much more... from success, more learning on what to do
better. And that knowledge shows _far_ more worth than any success will ever
seek to hit on your life.

Why? Because no one can take that away from you. That knowledge is _yours_ to
hold onto and grow from. And it shapes your life in profound ways.
